Dan Dan Noodles

Description

This delightful bowl of noodles, which hails from Sichuan cuisine, has a pleasing mouth-numbing quality thanks to the addition of Sichuan peppercorns. You can use pork mince, which is traditional, but we prefer it with a vegan mince such as This Isn't Pork or Beef mince, or even diced shiitake mushrooms.

You can adjust the spice level by increasing or decreasing the amount of chile crisp used (or omitting completely), though we recommend keeping the teaspoon of Sichuan peppercorns for an authentic flavour! It is also worth seeking out chinese sesame paste, which has a different flavour to tahini, though this can be substituted if necessary.

Ingredients

Pasta

  • 1 Tbsp. vegetable oil
  • 300g ground vegan pork alternative
  • 450g pappardelle
  • Kosher salt

Sauce

  • 1 tsp. Sichuan peppercorns
  • 1/2 tsp. five-spice powder
  • 2 Tbsp. Chinese sesame paste
  • 1 Tsbp. Chinkiang vinegar
  • 2 1/5 Tbsp. light soy sauce
  • 2 tsp. sugar
  • 3 Tbps. chile crisp e.g. Lao Gan Ma or similar, divided
  • 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped

To Serve

  • Handful peanuts, crushed
  • 2 spring onions, sliced
  • quarter cucumber, julienned (optional)

Steps

  1. Finely chop garlic cloves
  2. Toast peppercorns in a small skillet over medium high heat until slightly darkened and fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  3. Transfer to a spice mill or mortar and pestle and let cool Finely grind.
  4. Stir in a small bowl, then set aside:
    • ground Sichuan peppercorns
    • sesame paste
    • vinegar
    • sugar
    • 2/3 of the chile crisp
    • Soy sauce
  5. Meanwhile, heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high.
  6. Add to skillet:
    • pork
    • five-spice powder
    • 1 tsp. soy sauce
  7. Break meat up into pieces with a wooden spoon and cook, undisturbed, until it begins to brown, about 3 minutes.
  8. Stir and continue to cook, breaking up meat into smaller pieces until cooked through, about 2 minutes.
  9. Add garlic and ook, stirring, until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  10. Remove from heat and add Sichuan peppercorn mixture; Set aside.
  11. Cook pasta in a large pot of boiling salted water, stirring occasionally, until 1 minute short of al dente.
  12. Using tongs, transfer pasta to skillet with sauce and pour in 1/2 cup pasta cooking liquid.
  13. Bring to a simmer and cook, tossing gently until sauce is creamy and clings to pasta, about 1 minute.
  14. Drizzle in remaining 1/3 chile crisp.
  15. Divide pasta among plates and top with peanuts, spring onions and cucumber.
